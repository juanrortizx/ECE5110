\begin{abstract}
This report outlines an aspect of the yield analysis and optimization efforts undertaken by Forza Silicon and foundry partners throughout the image sensor production process. Beginning with design and wafer fabrication, the process includes detailed characterization, wafer probing, and final testing, with all image and data results stored locally for analysis. Through a new method using full wafer image mosaic at probe recurring issue were identified. One such issue was defective pixel error density increasing toward the wafer edge. The root cause was traced to contact resistance linked to oxidation at the pixel output stages. Collaborative efforts with the wafer fabrication vendor led to process modifications, including a new cleaning step and recipe adjustments. Comparative analysis between reference and modified wafers showed significant yield improvements and elimination of border defect patterns. Other refinements for this project addressed center die failures and color channel inconsistencies, particularly in the blue filter coating. These findings have been incorporated into the production flow, enhancing overall sensor reliability and yield. Forza Silicon continues to provide integrated production services with a strong focus on yield optimization.
\end{abstract}

\section{Context}
\label{sec:intro}
In the pursuit of high-performance image sensor production, yield optimization remains a critical challenge across the semiconductor industry. We present a detailed account of Forza Silicon’s collaborative efforts to enhance sensor yield through rigorous analysis and process refinement. Spanning the entire production lifecycle, from design and wafer fabrication to final testing, the study identifies recurring defect patterns and traces their root causes.

\section{Objective}
Through targeted simulations, probing, and vendor collaboration, we implemented modifications that significantly improved yield. This strongly correlates with the cost incurred in the manufacturing of CMOS image sensors. These findings underscore the importance of integrated diagnostics and vendor partnerships and demonstrate Forza Silicon’s commitment to continuous improvement in sensor reliability and manufacturing efficiency. 

\section{Method}
Beside standard semiconductor test wafer maps, we took advantage of the fact that each die can generate an image. Each of these images are analyzed independently to create a dedicated map (e.g. defect map, noise map). We aggregated the maps back into the wafer form keeping track of the physical orientation of the die within the wafer.

\section{Result}
Figure \ref{fig:1} illustrates the aggregated defect map corresponding to a low-yield wafer. The application of this processing methodology reveals an elevated spatial density of defective pixels, predominantly located at the wafer periphery.

Defining the edge of the wafer as the region of interest, we observe pairs of defective pixels noted in Figure \ref{fig:2} that are discernible in both dark and illuminated images. This defect signature has a correlation with the pixel output line readout architecture for this given pixel design, indicating that the defects originate from locations that are common to the corresponding shared pixel pairs.

\begin{figure}[H]

By correlating the precise pixel coordinates in the captured images with their corresponding locations in the design layout, we localized the defects to regions shared by diagonally adjacent pixels. This spatial mapping indicated that the shared via within the pixel structure Figure \ref{fig:3} was the underlying root cause of the observed artifacts.

\begin{figure}[H]

In collaboration with our partners at STMicro, this enabled a more detailed identification and characterization of the underlying issue. Using cross-sections Figure \ref{fig:4a} at this location, we compare measurements of a defective pixel Figure \ref{fig:4b} versus the same locations within a good pixel Figure \ref{fig:4c}, a higher oxidation was observed for the defective pixels producing a higher in-pixel resistance for these vias.
